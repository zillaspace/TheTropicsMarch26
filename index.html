02

03

Deal Structure

Acquisition mechanics first. Partnership terms second.

The LOI governs control, approvals, seller deferral, rent coverage, diligence, and the 99-year lease path. Partnership economics sit on top as a separate layer documented in the operating agreement.

LOI / Acquisition Layer

$8.0M purchase price. $500K upfront to Seller, credited to price and refundable until approvals. $3.5M deferred at 0% for the initial three-year lease term. 3-year term plus one 1-year extension ($200K extension fee). 60-day diligence period. Operational control first, purchase later.

Partnership / Investor Layer

$4.0M Investor + $500K LBHG. Investor receives 8% preferred return and return of capital first. LBHG contributes $500K funded equity (12.5%) plus a 12.5% sponsor promote. Post-refi, LBHG has a ROFR to buy up to 51% at FMV — aligning long-term control with execution.

Addendum A

Path to the 99-year lease

A step-by-step path to long-term lease security on Agua Caliente tribal trust land — a core structural pillar of the transaction.

Two-Phase Approach

A signed PSA or definitive purchase-option package is sufficient to begin groundwork immediately. The formal BIA application is tied to concurrent assignment and purchase-option exercise.

Phase 1: Groundwork (Years 1–2)

During the building lease, begin all preparatory work:

Get the TSR from BIA
Identify the landowner and build the relationship
Commission environmental assessment (NEPA)
Get the MAI appraisal for rent reasonableness
Pre-negotiate lease terms with the landowner
Request BIA Preliminary Review

None of this requires control of the ground lease. It is preparatory work that materially de-risks the formal application.

Phase 2: Formal Application (Year 3)

Begin the formal 99-year application ~12 months before target refi:

Exercise the purchase option
Harry assigns existing ground lease to LBHG entity
Submit full lease package to BIA (prep work done)
BIA approval within 30 days for complete packages
LTRO records the new 99-year lease
Apply for SBA/conventional refi with recorded lease

With groundwork complete, the formal application should be materially faster and cleaner than a cold-start process.

Key insight: Seller receives $500K upfront, LBHG covers the landlord-cost stack through the lease structure, and the remaining $3.5M carries at zero interest. That window is used to renovate, stabilize, and advance the 99-year lease file. By the time the purchase option is exercised, the BIA package is substantially prepared and the SBA refi can be paired with the acquisition takeout.

Addendum D

Why this structure wins

The economics favor patience

Harry has $4M of existing bank debt and $4M of equity. LBHG pays $500K upfront, services the $4M debt through rent, and leaves $3.5M at zero interest for three years — saving ~$720K versus market. Under an accelerated purchase, ~$12M in SBA debt from Day 1 implies $906–928K annual debt service against $162K Year 1 NOI. The structured path keeps effective debt service at ~$350K/year.

We don't own during the transition

Years 1–2 carry the most risk. Under this structure, exposure is limited to operating losses and invested capex — not the full $8M. The building lease allows the concept to be proven before ownership is assumed. This is the core investor protection.

The SBA conversation is different in Year 3

By Year 3, the lender evaluates a recorded 99-year lease, 12+ months of stabilized NOI at $1.6M+, a completed renovation, and a clean track record. That is a fundamentally stronger underwriting case than Year 1 projections. The result: cleaner credit, stronger proceeds, a more financeable refinance.

When accelerated purchase becomes the play

The accelerated path is logical if Seller prefers a faster exit. The LOI preserves optionality. Absent that signal, the structured acquisition remains stronger: lower risk, lower debt service through the J-curve, proven NOI at refinance, and ~$680–840K of interest savings.
